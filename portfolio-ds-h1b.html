The H-1B visa program in the United States has been a pivotal avenue for skilled foreign workers to contribute to the country's workforce. With millions of applications and a multitude of variables influencing grant decisions, understanding the factors that impact H-1B visa approvals is crucial. This project aims to delve into a comprehensive dataset spanning from 2017 to 2022, comprising 3.4 million data points sourced from the USCIS H-1B Employer Data Hub. By exploring this dataset, our objective is to uncover the key determinants affecting H-1B visa grants and construct a predictive model to forecast approval outcomes.

The motivation behind this project is multifaceted. Firstly, the H-1B visa program plays a critical role in attracting international talent to fulfill skill gaps in various industries. By comprehending the factors influencing visa approvals, we can assist both applicants and employers in optimizing their applications, potentially increasing the chances of successful grants.

Secondly, amidst the dynamic landscape of immigration policies and changing socio-economic environments, identifying trends and patterns within the H-1B visa data can offer insights into shifts in approval rates, preferred job sectors, geographical preferences, and other significant factors.

Lastly, the development of a prediction model holds immense practical value. If successful, such a model could serve as a valuable tool for stakeholders involved in the H-1B visa process, offering predictive guidance on potential outcomes based on application specifics. This predictive capability could streamline decision-making processes, providing applicants and employers with valuable insights to enhance their approach to visa applications.
